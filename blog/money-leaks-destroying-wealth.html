Money Leaks:
The Silent Wealth Killer

You earn ₹20 lakh. Yet ₹6-10k disappears monthly without a trace. Here's where it goes — and how to stop it.

The Math That Destroys Wealth

A ₹8,000/month leak is ₹96,000/year. Over 20 years, that's ₹19.2 lakh in wealth you never built. At 12% returns, it's ₹80 lakh in compound wealth lost.

Most high-earners don't notice leaks because:

  • Income is high enough to absorb them
  • Leaks are small individually (₹200 subscriptions, ₹500 food waste)
  • Recurring expenses hide in the background
  • No one is aggregating them and showing the total

The Four Leak Categories

1. Subscriptions (Invisible Bleeds)

Gym membership you don't use: ₹2,500/month. Netflix + Prime + YouTube + Spotify: ₹2,000/month. Cloud storage, productivity apps, streaming services: ₹1,500/month. Total: ₹6,000/month you forgot you were paying.

Action: List every recurring charge. Check bank statements for annual charges (audible, creative cloud). Delete everything unused. Typical savings: ₹3,000-6,000/month.

2. Lifestyle Inflation (The Invisible Rise)

Salary jumps from ₹15L to ₹20L. Your spend jumps from ₹80K to ₹110K. The ₹30K extra wasn't intentional — you just naturally started eating out more, taking cabs instead of metro, booking hotels instead of staying with cousins.

Over 10 years of career progression (₹15L→₹20L→₹28L→₹35L), lifestyle inflation absorbs 60-70% of each raise. You end up with ₹35L income but ₹2L/month spend, wondering where your wealth went.

3. True Debt Cost (Hidden Interest)

Your home loan EMI is ₹50,000/month. You think: "I'm paying ₹50K/month, so ₹6 lakh/year on my ₹60L salary is manageable." But the reality:

  • At 7% interest on 20-year loan, you're paying ₹30K interest, ₹20K principal monthly
  • First 5 years: mostly interest, minimal equity build
  • True cost of ₹50K EMI = ₹30K in lost wealth-building opportunity

4. Health & Lifestyle Drains (Invisible Costs)

Undiagnosed health issues: spending ₹500 on medicines monthly without solving the root cause. Food delivery instead of home-cooked meals: ₹2,000/month extra. Impulsive shopping during stress: ₹1,500/month.

How to Find Your Leaks

Step 1: Get last 3 months of bank and credit card statements

Step 2: Categorize every transaction (food, transport, subscriptions, entertainment, shopping)

Step 3: Sum each category. Identify spikes and recurring charges

Step 4: Question every subscription. If you can't recall using it in the last 30 days, cancel it

Step 5: Calculate total monthly leak. If > ₹5,000, you have a problem.

Real Impact

Professional earning ₹18 lakh/year discovers ₹8,400/month leak: ₹6,500 subscriptions + streaming, ₹1,900 food delivery inefficiency. Fixes in 1 week. Saves ₹100,800/year. At 12% growth, that's ₹40+ lakh in wealth in 20 years.
