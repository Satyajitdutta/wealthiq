## TAX OPTIMISATION (FY 2026-27)

### New Tax Regime Slabs
| Income Slab | Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to ₹4 lakh | 0% |
| ₹4L – ₹8L | 5% |
| ₹8L – ₹12L | 10% |
| ₹12L – ₹16L | 15% |
| ₹16L – ₹20L | 20% |
| ₹20L – ₹24L | 25% |
| Above ₹24L | 30% |

**Key**: 87A rebate — New regime total income ≤₹12L = NIL tax. Standard deduction ₹75,000.

### Old Regime — Key Deductions
- **80C**: ₹1,50,000 — ELSS, PPF, EPF, LIC premium, NSC, home loan principal, children's tuition
- **80CCD(1B)**: ₹50,000 extra for NPS Tier-1 (over and above 80C)
- **80D**: ₹25,000 self/family health insurance + ₹50,000 senior parents
- **80TTA**: ₹10,000 savings account interest
- **HRA**: Actual HRA or [Actual rent - 10% salary] or 40%/50% of basic — whichever is least
- **Home loan interest**: Up to ₹2L (Section 24b)

### Regime Decision Rule
- Annual income ≤₹12L → New regime almost always better (NIL tax with 87A).
- Income ₹12L–₹15L → Compare: if 80C + 80D + NPS fully utilised under old = better.
- Income >₹15L → Model both. If deductions exceed ₹3.75L, old regime may win.
- Switch threshold: Only recommend switching if saving ≥₹50,000/year.

## GOAL-BASED SIP PLANNING

### SIP Formula
Monthly SIP = FV × r / [(1+r)^n - 1]
Where: FV = future value, r = monthly return rate, n = months

### Standard Return Assumptions
| Asset | Expected CAGR |
|---|---|
| Large Cap Equity MF | 11–12% |
| Flexi/Multi Cap MF | 13–14% |
| Small/Mid Cap MF | 14–16% (higher risk) |
| ELSS | 12–13% |
| Debt MF | 7–8% |
| Hybrid (60:40) | 10–11% |
| PPF | 7.1% (guaranteed, tax-free) |
| NPS (50% equity) | 10–11% |
| EPF | 8.25% (guaranteed) |

### Goal Timelines → Asset Allocation
- **<3 years**: 80% debt, 20% liquid. No equity SIP.
- **3–5 years**: 50% hybrid, 30% large cap, 20% debt.
- **5–10 years**: 60% equity MF, 20% ELSS, 20% debt.
- **>10 years**: 70% equity (diversified), 20% ELSS, 10% NPS/PPF.

## INSURANCE GAP ANALYSIS

### Term Life Insurance
- **Needed**: 15–20x annual gross income (higher if home loan, young children, single earner)
- **Rule**: Reduce to 10x within 10 years of retirement (corpus built up by then)
- **Type**: Pure term only — no ULIPs, no endowment for protection needs
- **Riders worth considering**: Accidental death benefit, waiver of premium, critical illness

### Health Insurance
- **Minimum**: ₹5L per person; ₹10L+ in Tier 1 cities (medical inflation 8–10%/year)
- **Family floater**: Adequate for younger families (<45), individual recommended post-45
- **Top-up/Super top-up**: Most cost-effective way to increase coverage
- **What employer cover does NOT cover**: Pre-existing diseases (initial period), post-retirement

### Government Insurance Schemes (Highly underutilised)
| Scheme | Coverage | Premium | Eligibility |
|---|---|---|---|
| PMJJBY | ₹2L life cover | ₹436/year | 18–50 yrs, bank a/c |
| PMSBY | ₹2L accident cover | ₹20/year | 18–70 yrs, bank a/c |
| PMJAY (Ayushman) | ₹5L hospitalisation | Free | Income-based |
| Atal Pension Yojana | ₹1K–5K/month pension | ~₹200–₹1,400/month | 18–40, not IT assessee |

## RETIREMENT PLANNING — INDIA

### The Four Pillars
1. **EPF** (Employees' Provident Fund) — 12% employee + 12% employer, 8.25% p.a.
2. **NPS** (National Pension System) — market-linked, tax benefit 80CCD(1B) ₹50K extra
3. **PPF** (Public Provident Fund) — 7.1%, EEE tax status, max ₹1.5L/year, 15-year lock-in
4. **Equity MF SIPs** — wealth accumulation, highest long-term returns

### FIRE Number Calculation
- FIRE Number = Annual expenses at retirement × 25 (assumes 4% safe withdrawal rate)
- Adjust for inflation: use today's expenses × (1.06)^years to retirement
- Social security equivalent in India: EPF pension (EPS) + any government pension

### EPF Key Facts
- Employee contribution: 12% of basic salary
- Employer split: 3.67% to EPF + 8.33% to EPS (pension)
- Interest: 8.25% p.a. (FY 2025-26)
- Withdrawal: Tax-free after 5 continuous years of service
- EPS pension eligibility: 10 years of service, maximum pension at 58

### NPS Strategy
- Tier 1 (locked till 60): Tax deductible — use for 80CCD benefit
- Tier 2 (flexible): No tax benefit — not recommended over regular MF
- Asset allocation: 75% equity (E) max up to age 50, then auto-decreasing (lifecycle fund)
- At 60: 40% lump sum (tax-free), 60% mandatory annuity

## DEBT MANAGEMENT

### Priority Order for Extra Cash
1. Build 3–6 month emergency fund first (liquid MF or high-yield savings)
2. Pay off credit card debt (24–36% interest — guaranteed return)
3. Pay off personal loans (14–18% interest)
4. SIP in equity MF vs home loan prepayment — compare after-tax cost:
   - Home loan effective rate = interest rate × (1 - tax rate) if claiming deduction
   - If after-tax home loan rate < expected MF return → invest in MF
   - If after-tax home loan rate > expected MF return → prepay loan
